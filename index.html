Etymology Atlas guide

Etymology is represented as a directed evidence-backed multigraph, not as a simple family tree. Solid arrows show inherited descent, amber double lines show borrowing, green structural lines show derivation, and dashed red lines show uncertainty.

Scientific cautions

Cognates share an ancestor, but one cognate is not necessarily derived from another. Translation equivalence does not establish cognacy. Reconstructed forms are scholarly hypotheses and are marked with an asterisk. Dates are approximate where supplied. Absence from the current graph does not prove that a relationship does not exist.

Semantic zoom

At distant zoom, the atlas emphasises language branches and cognate hubs. At medium zoom, priority non-Latin nodes show native and Latin forms. Close and inspection zoom show both forms where reliable transliteration is available. The Word labels control can select Native + Latin, Native only, or Latin only without changing the underlying data. Inspection in the right panel always retains access to both forms, evidence, and licensing.

Controls

Drag to pan, use the wheel or trackpad to zoom, click to select, and double-click to focus and expand. Arrow keys move among nearby visible nodes. Enter focuses the selected node, Space pins it, and Escape closes menus or clears edge selection.

IE-CoR v1.2 corpus

The embedded IE-CoR v1.2 corpus was rebuilt from the complete verified official Zenodo archive and supports offline search, progressive loans and uncertainty, explorable language and meaning summaries, and reclaiming collapse. Cognate-set membership is not a direct word-to-word ancestry claim. Loan relations are shown only at the resolution supplied by the dataset. Branch summaries are application-generated view constructs, not historical entities.

IE-CoR v1.2 is licensed under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International. Citation: Heggarty, Paul, Cormac Anderson and Matthew Scarborough. 2024. IE-CoR v1.2. Zenodo. DOI 10.5281/zenodo.13304537.

Live sources

External sources default to Local only. Live retrieval requires an explicit action after selecting a local lexeme. Wikidata lemmata, forms, senses, statements, qualifiers and references remain distinct. Wiktionary is displayed only as bounded attributed evidence and is never converted automatically into graph relationships.
